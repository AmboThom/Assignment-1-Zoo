Real Life Dragons

Komodo dragons are the largest living lizards in the world. They can be identified by their massive size, flat heads, bowed legs and long, thick tails. Their name comes from rumors that a dragon-like creature lived on the Indonesian island of Komodo. Local people call them "ora," or "land crocodile."

Komodo dragons are carnivores and are fierce hunters who can eat very large prey, such as large water buffalo, deer, carrion, pigs and even humans. Komodo dragons can live for about 30 years in the wild. If an animal is lucky and escapes the jaws of a Komodo, it won't feel lucky for long. The dragon's saliva contains large amounts of bacteria, which poisons their victims, usually within 24 hours. Dragons will calmly follow their bitten prey for miles, using their keen sense of smell to find the corpse. They have huge appetites and have been known to eat up to 80 percent of their body weight in a single feeding.
